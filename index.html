05 Process Modelling — Order Fulfilment

A complementary business process modelling exercise on Northwind Trading's order fulfilment speed, prepared as a separate diagnostic from the checkout analysis above — included here for a fuller picture of the operational case study.

Business Activity Model

Northwind Trading's departments and their core activities, and how those activities connect.

As-Is Process State

Current order fulfilment process, modelled in BPMN — entirely sequential, with two manual coordination hand-offs between Warehouse, Administrator, and Delivery Driver.

To-Be Process State

Proposed process: two parallel gateways and two automated tasks shorten the critical path from ten sequential steps to seven, without adding headcount.

⚙ Automated task — generated by a software solution rather than created manually ◆ Parallel gateway — paths on either side proceed at the same time
Recommendation

Fix the checkout before the next reporting cycle

Traffic is not at risk — conversion is. Four changes, ranked by complaint share, address 100% of what customers are telling Northwind Trading.

53% of complaints

Reinstate guest checkout and make address fields optional where a house number doesn't apply.

20% of complaints

Revisit the new layout — usability-test the redesign against the old flow before further rollout.

13% of complaints

Improve product categorisation and rewrite vague product descriptions.

14% of complaints

Add a payment method beyond credit card; finish Ali's image-speed optimisation.
